"With the Youth" Advocacy

It’s not enough to prepare young people to see and interact with the world differently; we also need to prepare the world to see and interact with young people differently

  • Signaling to change for the better
  • "A shared future"

Whoever determines what can be talked about also determines what can be known. Dialogue being structured by institutions that exclude youth voices dictate how youth think, what they do and what they are.

Today, we often hear adults in government and non-government institutions using the phrase “for the youth” in youth empowerment programs. “We will support sports for the youth”, “we will make jobs for the youth.” This sidelines youth to be onlookers and only wait for what the adults have to deliver to them.

SftG says NO to this. We advocate for equal partnership under the “with the youth” campaign, where adults simultaneously work with youth in vital decision-making positions in society, because we believe there is no youth development and youth empowerment without the active participation of the youth themselves.

In other words, there can be no development without the participation of the stakeholders involved. It is by questioning, expressing their views, and having the opportunity to participate that young people develop skills, confidence and enhance potential well-being.

The SftG initiative does not only equip youth with change-making and workplace skills and opportunities, but also breaks down barriers that keep too many of the world’s young people from realizing their full potential. With schools, communities, governments, nonprofits, and the private sector, SftG is shaping a world where adults not only value young people but seek their input. Therefore, when young women and men have the same decision-making opportunities as adults, they not only elevate themselves, but also their families, communities, and whole ecosystem.

SftG advocates for “with the youth” because we believe the future is a shared one. Indeed, the involvement of adults is key to the success of youth empowerment. Adult allies are responsible for creating an empowering group setting in which their young people can engage.

The empowerment happens to be assured when youth come to realize that there is enhancement in their abilities to control, influence or cope with their socio-economic roles. It not only covers economic empowerment but also takes social, ideological, educational, technological and political empowerment in its ambit.
