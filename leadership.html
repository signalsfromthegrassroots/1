Dialogue to Action

At SftG, we believe that young people who discover and exercise their potentials are problem solvers, not problems to be solved

  • Signaling to change for the better
  • "Mutual empowerment"

Dialogue is the very heart of shaping our society. It is the starting point for many meaningful, sustainable and beneficial collaborations that bring greater growth and development in the quest for positive change.

However, despite youth having the will, passion and capability to make change, many of them lack the skill of holding an effective dialogue – something that is holding them back from transforming their passions into meaningful action.
This is a big threat in the struggle to ensure more youth engagement in decision-making processes. SftG aims to address this incompetence by building a culture of dialogue among young people.

Developing youths' dialoging skills has always been near and dear to the heart of everyone at SftG. We aim to deepen youths' understanding of dialogue as a critical aspect of constructive engagement and action by providing them a safe platform where they can share their experiences, knowledge and shed light on their projects.

Our dialogues are never events, but continuous processes in which youth get inspiration, motivation and knowledge to develop themselves and keep driving their actions of change. In these dialogues, Signals interact through speaking and listening to one another to understand the issues and challenges that they face, and together formulate ways of solving them.

Why dialogue?

How we talk about things matters. At SftG, it is the way we dialogue that creates a safe space for youth to discuss conflicting issues, understand and respect the values and beliefs of one another.

The very definition of dialogue is why SftG centralizes it. Dialogue is defined as a space where participants; -

1. Respectfully express their opinion.
2. Civilly acknowledge those who hold different beliefs from own.
3. Not aim at coming to agreement but to create a common understanding and appreciation of the different viewpoints that exist.
4. Look beyond differences, gain skills and knowledge to resolve conflict, and be active citizens and responsible members of society.
5. Aim to understand and be understood by others by giving undivided attention.
6. Build relationships.
7. Uncover multiple perspectives.
8. Collectively improve critical and creative thinking skills.
9. Work together to solve problems.
10. Let go of the “us versus them” mentality.
11. Be non-judgmental.
12. Use empathetic listening and silence effectively.

These ideals make the SftG environment safe and favourable for youth to fully express themselves as well as discover and exercise their potentials which transforms them into problem solvers.

How does it happen?

In an era of constant technological change and new platforms of communication, the space and means for dialogue are increasing and it is up to us to utilize them. SftG creates channels—both online and in person—for young people to drive conversations. We use zoom and social media platforms like facebook and Instagram to work directly with young people to elevate their stories.

Every month, we hold atleast one dialogue for each SDG. This means that for a month with three SDGs, the least number of dialogues is three. We arrange these SDGs in such a way that the dialogue of a given SDG connects to that of the next, as shown below.

Walking the Talk

SftG is much aware that convening is half the battle.

Based on our dialogues, we know for certain that the world’s young people are eager to secure a sustainable future. However, we need to not only listen to their ideas and perspectives but get them manifested in a way that they truly effect the course of events in the world. We walk the talk through the following ways; -

1. SftG dedicates time and energy to drive young peoples’ voices through building partnerships and working collaboratively with stakeholders and leaders. These partnerships provide means of amplifying Signals’ voices by availing opportunities to bring youths' issues and opinions to the attention of individuals in decision-making positions. They also avail opportunity for Signals to directly engage and dialogue with stakeholders - something that builds strong and mutual beneficial relationships with decision-makers.
Partnerships further avail community engagement opportunities like volunteerings and internships which youth utilize to put their dialoging skills into action and to also inspire colleagues whom they get to encounter there.

2. While drawing information from Signals’ experiences, insights, and enthusiasm, SftG formulates policies and practices, that focus on the ‘connective tissue’ in the workforce system. These include formulating improved hiring and recruitment practices, reducing barriers to employment, and creating equitable policies. We then share these formulated practices to concerned entities in hope that they will adopt them and recognize the importance of meaningful participation of young people in all development processes across their boards.

3. We appeal to companies, government and non-government institutions to increase the employability of youth in their crucial decision-making positions with the explanation that this will make their systems more relevant, inclusive, and responsive to youths’ real aspirations, challenges, and needs.

4. SftG implements intergenerational collaboration by involving adults in its dialogues. This establishes a starting point of getting adults to listen to young people and seek their input.
